Technical Notes -- uu , u

high back vowel

Short <u> and long <uu> both represent a high back vowel [u]. When it occurs between velar consonants, the phoneme /u/ may be lowered to [o], in which case it is generally written <o> rather than <u>. Thus, note the difference in the prestem vowel in the following examples.

naxghogdeg 'I should tell a story'
naxghuldeg 'you should tell a story'
