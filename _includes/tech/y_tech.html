Tanacross yh is a voiceless palatal approximant (sonorant) [ç ]. While it may sometimes be produced with slight friction, it patterns phonologically as an approximant, not a fricative. It is restricted to syllable-final position.

Tanacross y is a voiced palatal approximant [j]. It may occur in both onset and coda positions.

The contrast between yh and its voiced counterpart y can be seen in pairs xeyh 'spruce roots' versus xey 'winter'.

At the end of a syllable y may occur lengthened, written yy.

sh'ǎayy'   'my snowshoes'
